# Expert outreach — "underused ideas for public safety in New York City"

A short, sendable note to a diverse group of 10 to 20 experts, to surface
underused and underinvested ideas the literature scan might miss. Their answers can
be cross-checked against the radar and folded into a themed series.

---

## Draft email

**Subject:** Your top 3 underused public-safety ideas for New York City?

Hi [Name],

I am working on a Vital City project that hunts for scalable, cost-effective ideas to
improve public safety in New York City — with a particular interest in approaches that are
well supported by evidence but underused or underinvested, rather than the usual suspects.

I would be grateful for your quick take on one question:

**What are your top three underused or underinvested ideas for improving public safety in
New York City?**

For each, a sentence or two is plenty. If it is easy, a pointer to a study or program you
find convincing would help, but it is not required.

I am asking a small, deliberately diverse group, and I will share what emerges. No need for
anything polished — your instinct is exactly what I am after.

Thank you,
Josh

## How to use the results
- Tally which ideas recur across experts; recurrence is a signal in itself.
- Map each nominated idea to a radar theme (reentry, domestic violence, alcohol and
  addiction, lead, schools, and so on).
- Flag ideas experts raise that the scan did not surface — those are gaps worth a manual
  literature search.
- Use the strongest recurring themes to shape the bundles for a series.
