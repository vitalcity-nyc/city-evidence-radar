How this works

Each weekly run pulls the newest items from the sources above, then an AI model (Claude) reads the title and abstract of each and scores it on four dimensions you can see on every card: causal strength (is the evidence experimental or quasi-experimental?), scalability, cost-effectiveness, and NYC applicability. Public-safety ideas are weighted up. The overall score (0–100) blends these, with a bonus for stronger research designs.

The default view shows only studies rated as having causal evidence (randomized or strong quasi-experimental designs). Turn that filter off to see the full field, including descriptive and observational work.

About the score

Scores are a triage aid, not a verdict. They are generated from abstracts only — not full papers — so they can misjudge a study's true design, cost, or relevance. Always read the linked paper before acting on or publishing anything. The causal-strength label is a heuristic classification, and a paper’s real-world cost depends heavily on local context.

⚠ A note on AI in this tool

This radar uses an AI model to triage research. Please keep in mind:

  • Every summary, score, method note and causal-strength label is AI-generated from the abstract only, not the full paper. Treat them as a starting point, not ground truth.
  • The AI can misread findings, overstate or understate cost and scalability, or mis-classify a study’s research design.
  • Relevance, scalability, cost and NYC-applicability ratings are judgment calls the model makes — reasonable people (and the authors) may disagree.
  • Coverage is partial: SSRN and several think tanks block automated access and are not included (see footer).
  • Always open and read the linked study before citing, publishing, or acting on any idea here.
